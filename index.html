Direction — RTL · D-13

Hit flip at the top of this page. Every component above mirrors and nothing else about it changes — that is the whole gate, and it is run against these bytes rather than a duplicate page, because a duplicate is free to drift out of agreement with what it certifies.
Three rules hold it:
1 · Nothing here has a side. Layout is flex, grid and shorthand padding, so mirroring is the browser's job, not ours. There were no margin-left-style properties in tokens.css to convert — the conversion found nothing, which is the design holding, not a step skipped.
2 · Direction is one token, in one file. An angle is physical and CSS has no to inline-end, so gradients read var(--flow) and tokens.css flips it once. A gradient that hard-codes 90deg will silently refuse to mirror, and silence is why this is a token rather than a convention.
3 · Machine strings isolate. Hex, DID, hash and GRADE are LTR content whose edge characters are neutrals. Unisolated on an RTL page, #45C2DC displays as 45C2DC# — the DOM value is intact and only the display is wrong, which is precisely the failure a human eyeballing an identifier cannot catch.
Deliberately not mirrored: the check, shield and person glyphs. A checkmark does not reverse for an RTL reader, and mirroring icons on principle is how you end up with a backwards tick.
Where the line falls. Flip this page and the English sentences show their full stop at the wrong end — …legible. renders as .legible…. That is the same neutral-resolution mechanism as the hex case, and it is left alone on purpose. The rule: isolate what will never be translated, leave what will be. A hex, a DID and a hash are the same bytes in every locale, so they must never take direction from their surroundings. Prose is the opposite — in a real RTL build these sentences are Arabic, the paragraph is genuinely RTL, and the stop lands correctly with no help. Isolating prose would hard-code English's direction into the one layer that is supposed to change. English text under dir="rtl" is an artificial state that exists only for this fixture; rtl-smoke.html is where real RTL script is tested.
